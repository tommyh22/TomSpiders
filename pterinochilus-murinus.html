Pterinochilus murinus

The Orange Baboon Tarantula (OBT)

Quick Facts

  • Scientific name: Pterinochilus murinus
  • Common name: Orange Baboon Tarantula (OBT)
  • Native to: Central & Southern Africa
  • Temperament: Extremely defensive and fast
  • Growth rate: Fast
  • Lifestyle: Heavy webber with fossorial tendencies

Care Sheet

Temperature: 24–28°C

Humidity: 50–70% (good ventilation, don’t keep it swampy)

Enclosure: Dry substrate with plenty of anchor points for webbing, cork bark, and hides.

Feeding: Greedy eater; suitable-sized crickets/roaches 1–2 times per week for juveniles, less for adults.

Temperament

This species is nicknamed the “Orange Bitey Thing” for a reason. They are very fast, extremely defensive, and will not hesitate to threat pose or strike.

Not recommended for beginners. Enjoy them as a display species – no handling.
